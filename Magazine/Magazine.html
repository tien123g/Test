Apple History

Of the Curriculum

  • 1971-1985: Jobs and Wozniak

    Apple I and company formation

    The two Steves made a last trip to the Homebrew Computer Club and demonstrated the Apple I (AKA: The Apple Computer). Paul Terrell, who operated the computer store chain Byte Shop, was impressed, and gave the two Steves his card, asking them to keep in touch.

  • 1985-1997: Sculley, Spindler, Amelio

    Corporate performance

    Apple continued to sell both lines of its computers, the Apple II and the Macintosh. A few months after introducing the Mac, Apple released a compact version of the Apple II called the Apple IIc.

  • 1997-2001: Apple's comeback

    Return of Steve Jobs

    On November 10, 1997, Apple introduced the Apple Store, an online retail store based upon the WebObjects application server the company had acquired in its purchase of NeXT. The new direct sales outlet was tied to a new build-to-order manufacturing strategy
